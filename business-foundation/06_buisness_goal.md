# Business Goal

---

## Revenue Timeline

**Month 1–3 (Fast Revenue)**
- Goal: Secure 1st paid client
- Revenue target: €500–1,500 (1–2 small projects)
- Focus: Prove you can deliver, build case study

**Month 4–6**
- Goal: 2–4 clients with monthly retainers
- Revenue target: €1,500–3,500/month (mix of projects + retainers)
- Focus: Systematize your delivery, build referral pipeline

**Month 7–12**
- Goal: 5–8 active clients, sustainable business
- Revenue target: €3,000–6,000/month
- Focus: Scale without burning out, hire if needed

---

## Pricing Model

### Project-Based (Initial Revenue)
- **Small automation** (1–2 integrations): €300–600
- **Medium automation** (3–5 integrations, some logic): €800–1,500
- **Complex automation** (5+ integrations, custom logic): €2,000–4,000

**Timeline:** Most projects take 2–4 weeks (you're learning)

### Monthly Retainer (Recurring Revenue)
- **Basic support:** €150–300/month (monitoring, minor updates, troubleshooting)
- **Full maintenance:** €400–800/month (optimization, scaling, new integrations)

**Goal:** Move clients from 1-time projects → retainers after 3 months

---

## Quick-Win Strategy (Months 1–3)

**First client should be:**
1. **Simple** (1–2 integrations, clear ROI)
2. **Fast** (deliverable in 2–3 weeks)
3. **Visible** (easy to show the impact)

**Example first project:**
- Local business → Lead + Email + Google Sheets sync
- Saves them 5–10 hours/week
- Cost: €500, ROI visible in 2 weeks

**Why?** You need proof you can deliver. Case studies > promises.

---

## Success Metrics

- **Month 1:** 1st client acquisition (doesn't matter if small)
- **Month 2:** Delivery (client sees the automation working)
- **Month 3:** Referral or 2nd client
- **Month 6:** 50% of revenue from recurring retainers
- **Month 12:** 70% from retainers, minimal project churn

---

## Operational Goals

1. **Delivery:** Complete projects on time, first try (quality > speed initially)
2. **Communication:** Clear expectations, weekly updates, responsive
3. **Documentation:** Every workflow documented so clients (& you) understand it
4. **Iteration:** After each project, improve your process (faster next time)
